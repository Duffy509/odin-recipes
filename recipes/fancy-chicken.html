Clancey's Fancy Chicken


This wonderful dish is easy to make and high on protein. The simple combination of just six ingredients makes a delicious and healthy dinner with just a hint of heat. This recipe was originally invented by my great great grandmother's gerbil, Hattori Hanzo, in 1843. Hattori grew up in New York and ran his own gerbil restaurant for many years. Hattori first discovered hot and spicy dishes on a trip to Papua New Guinea, where he studied under the head chef of the Fore people. It truly is a miracle that the recipes survided until today

Ingredients

    1 Boneless Skinless Chicken Breast
    1 tbsp Olive Oil
    1 tsp Worchestershire Sauce
    1 tsp Lemon Juice
    1 tbsp Sriracha
    1/2 cup Rice of choice (dry)

Optional Garnishes

    1 or 2 Diced Green Onions
    Sesame Seeds

Cooking Instructions

    Cook rice per package instructions, complete rest of recipe while cooking, remove from heat when done
    Dice chicken into 1" cubes
    Add olive oil to pan, heat on medium to high heat
    Fry chicken in pan until lightly browned on all sides
    Add Worchestershire sauce to pan, toss chicken in it
    Add lime juice to pan, deglaze and browned bits stuck to pan
    Add Sriracha, toss chicken in it
    Serve in a bowl, top the rice with the chicken and optional garnishes if desired
